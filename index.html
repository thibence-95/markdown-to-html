If you ever spot me staring into the distance, this is probably what I'm thinking about:

It was the end of July 2012, My dad and I went to visit friends in NYC for a week. Going there was an amazing experience.
I went out to local skateparks and shredded around the city streets on my trusty skateboard.
When I was at LES Skatepark underneath Manhattan Bridge. IT HAPPENED. After 2 years of trying oh so hard
I finally landed my first ever Kick-flip (a basic,yet cool, skateboard trick) I had people turning heads when I shouted "F* YES, I DID IT!".
For the rest of the trip I brought my skateboard everywhere I could, popping Kick-flips left, right and centre.

When we got back to Belgium I met up with some of my friends A.S.A.P to show them I could finally pull it off but suddenly I no longer could so my friends didn't believe me.

I haven't landed the bastard trick ever since. My friends and I have slowly stopped skating by now and they still think it's all horse shit to this day.
